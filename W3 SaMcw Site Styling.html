When Did You Stop Loving Us?
by Sarah McWhite

When did you stop loving us?
When did the darts
Propelled to inject us with amnesia
About the beauty of dark skin
Infect you?
When did you forget it was black knees
Between which your scalp was soothed
And your secret tears collected
When the others tried to trample you
Like mire beneath their feet
And it got you?
Don't you remember that
You were at home
In the safety of the shadow of our bosom?
Do you recall how we fought alongside you
Not against you
And cleaned your battle scars with
Bay rum and molasses.
And when it was hardest to breathe love
Because they polluted our atmosphere with hate
We loved you with all the strength
We are all forced to have to survive.

But they made your eyes blurry.
You can't see our beauty.
They made you despise yourself so deep
You cover your mirrors
And any likeness of the true you
Must be kept at arm's length.

When?
When did you stop loving us?
When did your soul imbibe
The words of our enemies
Making you spit them back at us
Like poisoned darts?
When?
When black men?
When did you stop loving us?
